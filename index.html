Research Publications

Multi Robot Environment Exploration Using Swarm Springer, Singapore · Jan 4, 2022
View Publication
 Abstract:

This chapter discusses the exploration of unknown environments using multiple robots to address the inefficiencies of single-robot systems. Single robots are inefficient and inaccurate for mapping large unknown areas, leading to increased exploration time and errors. To improve this, we developed a system using multiple robots and the Random-Exploration Random Tree (RRT) algorithm. The robots communicate and coordinate through a swarm algorithm, preventing them from exploring the same area twice, thus saving time and enhancing mapping accuracy. The system is implemented using ROS middleware and tested with the GAZEBO simulator. This multi-robot approach is poised to be beneficial for future smart city ecosystems.

Mobile Robot Navigation and Mapping International Journal of Creative Research Thoughts · Jul 7, 2020
View Publication
 Abstract:

This paper proposes a control architecture of a mobile robot which is capable of performing various task such as cleaning of the house as a cleaning robot. The key idea behind the working of these robots is simultaneous localization and mapping. The mobile robot uses an ultrasonic sensor to avoid unexpected obstacle detection in the environment and map the environment. Along with Ultrasonic sensors we have used Encoders which will give us the tic values with the help of which robot can map the environment by detecting its simultaneous position. In this paper, we have described the mapping and obstacle avoidance algorithm in detail.

Simultaneous Traffic Management System International Journal of Engineering Research & Technology · May 5, 2020

View Publication
 Abstract:

With the surge in vehicle numbers, urban traffic has become a critical issue worldwide. Current systems struggle to manage this influx effectively, resulting in inefficient traffic control. To address this, an integrated detection and tracking approach is vital for real-time operation. We propose a cost-effective camera-based algorithm for traffic flow management. The algorithm comprises vehicle detection, counting, and tracking phases. Background subtraction isolates vehicles, Kalman filter tracks them, and the Hungarian algorithm associates labels with tracked vehicles. Tested on both daytime and nighttime CCTV and IR camera footage, experimental results demonstrate the algorithm's effectiveness in traffic management.
